## Wheat (गेहूँ)

### Recommended Varieties by Zone
- **North-Western Plains (Punjab, Haryana, W. UP):** HD-2967, PBW-343, DBW-17, WH-542
- **North-Eastern Plains (E. UP, Bihar):** HD-2733, NW-1014, K-307
- **Central Zone (MP, Rajasthan, Gujarat):** GW-366, HI-1544, MP-3336
- **Peninsular Zone (Maharashtra, Karnataka):** NIAW-34, HD-2189

### Sowing Protocol
- **Optimal sowing window:** October 25 – November 25 (irrigated), November 1 – December 15 (rainfed)
- **Seed rate:** 100 kg/ha (normal), 125 kg/ha (late sown)
- **Seed treatment:** Carboxin 75% WP @ 2.5 g/kg seed OR Trichoderma viride @ 4 g/kg seed
- **Row spacing:** 20–22.5 cm (normal), 15–18 cm (late sown for higher plant population)
- **Seed depth:** 5–6 cm

### Fertilizer Schedule (per hectare)
- **Basal (at sowing):** 60 kg N + 60 kg P₂O₅ + 40 kg K₂O + 25 kg ZnSO₄
- **First top dressing (21 DAS, CRI stage):** 60 kg N
- **Second top dressing (42 DAS, tillering):** 30 kg N (if yield target >50 q/ha)
- **Micronutrients:** Foliar spray of 0.5% ZnSO₄ + 0.25% Boric Acid at flag leaf stage

### Irrigation Schedule
- **Critical stages:** Crown Root Initiation (21 DAS), Tillering (40 DAS), Late Jointing (60 DAS), Flowering (80 DAS), Milk Stage (100 DAS), Dough Stage (115 DAS)
- **Total irrigations:** 4–6 depending on soil type and rainfall
- **Water requirement:** 350–450 mm total
- **Deficit priority:** If only 1 irrigation available → CRI stage. If 2 → CRI + Flowering. If 3 → CRI + Late Jointing + Flowering.

### Major Diseases
- **Yellow Rust (Puccinia striiformis):** Yellow-orange pustules on leaves in linear pattern. Favored by 10–15°C with high humidity. Spray Propiconazole 25% EC @ 0.1% at first appearance.
- **Brown Rust (Puccinia triticina):** Orange-brown pustules scattered on upper leaf surface. Spray Mancozeb 75% WP @ 0.25%.
- **Karnal Bunt (Tilletia indica):** Partial conversion of grain to black powder. Seed treatment with Carboxin. No effective field spray.
- **Loose Smut (Ustilago tritici):** Entire ear replaced by black spore mass. Seed treatment with Carboxin 75% WP @ 2.5 g/kg.
- **Powdery Mildew (Erysiphe graminis):** White powdery growth on leaves. Spray Sulfur 80% WP @ 0.3%.

### Major Pests
- **Termites:** Soil application of Chlorpyrifos 20% EC @ 5 L/ha with irrigation water. (Note: Check CIBRC status before recommending.)
- **Aphids:** Spray Dimethoate 30% EC @ 1 L/ha or Imidacloprid 17.8% SL @ 100 ml/ha when >10 aphids/ear.
- **Pink Stem Borer:** Use pheromone traps. Spray Cartap Hydrochloride 50% SP @ 1 kg/ha.

### Harvest
- **Maturity indicators:** Grain hard, golden yellow color, moisture <14%
- **Optimal harvest window:** 5–7 days after physiological maturity
- **Yield potential:** 40–55 q/ha (irrigated), 15–25 q/ha (rainfed)

## Paddy / Rice (धान)

### Recommended Varieties
- **Basmati:** Pusa-1121, Pusa-1509, CSR-30 (saline tolerant)
- **Non-Basmati (short duration):** IR-64, Sona Masuri, MTU-7029
- **Flood tolerant:** Swarna-Sub1, Sahbhagi Dhan
- **Direct Seeded Rice (DSR):** Pusa-44, PR-126

### Nursery Management
- **Nursery area:** 800–1000 m² per hectare of main field
- **Seed rate:** 30 kg/ha (transplanted), 40–50 kg/ha (DSR)
- **Seed treatment:** Carbendazim 50% WP @ 2 g/kg seed (against blast)
- **Nursery duration:** 21–25 days (short duration), 30–35 days (long duration)
- **Seedlings per hill:** 2–3

### Transplanting
- **Spacing:** 20 cm × 15 cm (general), 20 cm × 10 cm (hybrid)
- **Depth:** 3–4 cm, avoid deep planting
- **Optimal age:** 21–25 day old seedlings, 3–4 leaf stage
- **Water management:** 5 cm standing water for first 2 weeks, then alternate wetting and drying (AWD)

### Fertilizer Schedule (per hectare)
- **Basal:** 40 kg N + 60 kg P₂O₅ + 40 kg K₂O + 25 kg ZnSO₄
- **First top dressing (21 DAT, active tillering):** 40 kg N
- **Second top dressing (42 DAT, panicle initiation):** 40 kg N
- **Total N for hybrid:** 150 kg/ha

### Major Diseases
- **Blast (Pyricularia oryzae):** Diamond-shaped lesions on leaves, neck rot. Favored by 20–25°C, high humidity, excess N. Spray Tricyclazole 75% WP @ 0.06% or Isoprothiolane 40% EC @ 1.5 ml/L.
- **Bacterial Leaf Blight (Xanthomonas oryzae):** Water-soaked lesions from leaf tip, turning yellow-white. No effective chemical control. Use resistant varieties. Avoid excess N.
- **Sheath Blight (Rhizoctonia solani):** Oval greenish-grey lesions on sheath near water level. Spray Hexaconazole 5% SC @ 2 ml/L or Validamycin 3% SL @ 2 ml/L.
- **False Smut (Ustilaginoidea virens):** Large velvety green spore balls on individual grains. Spray Propiconazole 25% EC @ 1 ml/L at boot leaf stage.
- **Brown Spot (Helminthosporium oryzae):** Oval brown spots on leaves. Indicates potassium/zinc deficiency. Correct nutrient deficiency + Mancozeb 75% WP @ 2.5 g/L.

### Major Pests
- **Stem Borer (Scirpophaga incertulas):** Dead heart in vegetative, white ear in reproductive stage. Use pheromone traps (5/ha). Spray Cartap Hydrochloride 50% SP @ 1 kg/ha.
- **Brown Plant Hopper (BPH):** Hopperburn — circular patches of dried plants. Avoid excess N. Drain water for 3 days. Spray Pymetrozine 50% WG @ 0.3 g/L.
- **Leaf Folder:** Leaves folded longitudinally with larva inside. Spray Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC @ 0.3 ml/L.

### Water Management
- **Total requirement:** 1200–1500 mm (puddled transplanted), 800–1000 mm (DSR)
- **AWD technique:** Irrigate when water drops 15 cm below soil surface (use field water tube). Saves 15–25% water.
- **Critical stages:** Transplanting → Active Tillering → Panicle Initiation → Flowering → Grain Filling

## Potato (आलू)

### Recommended Varieties
- **Plains (UP, Bihar, W. Bengal):** Kufri Jyoti, Kufri Pukhraj, Kufri Bahar, Kufri Chandramukhi
- **Hills:** Kufri Giriraj, Kufri Himalini
- **Processing (chips):** Kufri Chipsona-1, Kufri Chipsona-3

### Planting Protocol
- **Season:** October–November (plains), February–March (hills)
- **Seed rate:** 25–30 q/ha (whole tubers), 15–20 q/ha (cut seed)
- **Seed size:** 30–40 g tubers preferred
- **Spacing:** 60 cm × 20 cm (row × plant)
- **Depth:** 5–7 cm, followed by earthing up at 30 and 45 DAP

### Fertilizer Schedule (per hectare)
- **Basal:** 150 kg N + 80 kg P₂O₅ + 100 kg K₂O + 25 kg ZnSO₄
- **Note:** Apply full P, K, and Zn at planting. Split N: 75 kg basal + 75 kg at first earthing up (30 DAP)

### Major Diseases
- **Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans):** Dark water-soaked lesions on leaves, white fungal growth underneath. Spreads rapidly in cool (15–20°C), humid, foggy weather. **Most destructive potato disease in India.** Spray Mancozeb 75% WP @ 2.5 g/L as preventive (before symptoms). Spray Cymoxanil 8% + Mancozeb 64% WP @ 3 g/L as curative. Repeat every 7 days during active disease period.
- **Early Blight (Alternaria solani):** Concentric ring ("target board") lesions on lower leaves. Spray Mancozeb 75% WP @ 2.5 g/L.
- **Black Scurf (Rhizoctonia solani):** Black crusty patches on tuber surface. Seed treatment with Boric acid 3% solution for 30 minutes.
- **Common Scab (Streptomyces scabies):** Rough corky patches on tuber skin. Maintain soil moisture during tuber initiation. Avoid alkaline soils.

### Major Pests
- **Cutworm:** Cuts young plants at soil level at night. Soil drench with Chlorpyrifos 20% EC @ 4–5 L/ha. (Check CIBRC status.)
- **Aphids (Myzus persicae):** Vector for viral diseases. Spray Imidacloprid 17.8% SL @ 0.3 ml/L.
- **Potato Tuber Moth:** Mines in leaves and tubers. Use pheromone traps. Spray Quinalphos 25% EC @ 2 ml/L.

### Harvest & Storage
- **Dehaulming:** Cut haulms 10–15 days before harvest (helps skin hardening)
- **Maturity:** 75–90 days (early), 90–120 days (main crop)
- **Storage:** Cold store at 2–4°C, 90–95% RH. For processing: 8–12°C.
- **Yield potential:** 250–350 q/ha (irrigated)

## Cotton (कपास)

### Recommended Varieties
- **Bt Cotton (most of India):** Bollgard-II hybrids (check state-approved list)
- **Desi Cotton:** AKA-5, Jayadhar
- **ELS (Extra Long Staple):** Suvin, DCH-32

### Sowing Protocol
- **Season:** April–May (irrigated), June–July (rainfed, after onset of monsoon)
- **Seed rate:** 2.5 kg/ha (Bt hybrid), 15–20 kg/ha (desi)
- **Spacing:** 90–120 cm × 60–90 cm (varies by region)
- **Seed treatment:** Imidacloprid 70% WS @ 5 g/kg seed (against sucking pests)

### Major Diseases
- **Bacterial Blight (Xanthomonas citri pv. malvacearum):** Angular water-soaked spots on leaves. Spray Streptocycline @ 0.01% + Copper Oxychloride 50% WP @ 0.3%.
- **Fusarium Wilt:** Yellowing and wilting from lower leaves. No chemical control. Use resistant varieties and crop rotation.
- **Grey Mildew (Ramularia areola):** White powdery growth on lower leaf surface. Spray Carbendazim 50% WP @ 1 g/L.

### Major Pests
- **Bollworms (American, Pink, Spotted):** Use pheromone traps (5/ha). For non-Bt: Spray Emamectin Benzoate 5% SG @ 0.4 g/L. Bt cotton has in-built protection against American Bollworm.
- **Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci):** Vector for Cotton Leaf Curl Virus (CLCuV). Spray Pyriproxyfen 10% EC @ 1 ml/L or Spiromesifen 22.9% SC @ 0.8 ml/L.
- **Jassids:** Yellowing of leaf margins, downward curling. Spray Flonicamid 50% WG @ 0.3 g/L.
- **Pink Bollworm:** Use mass trapping with pheromone traps (40/ha). Spray Profenophos 50% EC @ 2 ml/L at 50% flowering.

## Sugarcane (गन्ना)

### Recommended Varieties
- **Uttar Pradesh:** Co-0238, CoS-767, CoJ-64
- **Maharashtra:** Co-86032, CoM-0265
- **Karnataka:** Co-86032, CoC-671

### Planting Protocol
- **Season:** October (autumn), February–March (spring)
- **Setts:** 3-budded setts, 35,000–40,000 setts/ha
- **Spacing:** 75–90 cm row spacing
- **Sett treatment:** Dip in Carbendazim 50% WP @ 2 g/L for 15 min (against red rot)
- **Intercropping:** Wheat/mustard/potato in autumn planted cane (first 3–4 months)

### Fertilizer Schedule (per hectare)
- **Basal:** 60 kg N + 80 kg P₂O₅ + 60 kg K₂O
- **First top dressing (45 DAP):** 60 kg N
- **Second top dressing (90 DAP):** 60 kg N
- **Third top dressing (120 DAP):** 30 kg N (for high yield)
- **Total N:** 210 kg/ha (plant cane), 250 kg/ha (ratoon)

### Major Diseases
- **Red Rot (Colletotrichum falcatum):** Red discoloration of internal tissue with white transverse bands. No chemical control. Use resistant varieties. Hot water treatment of setts (50°C for 2 hours).
- **Smut (Ustilago scitaminea):** Black whip-like structure from top of plant. Remove and burn infected plants. Use resistant varieties.
- **Wilt (Fusarium sacchari):** Gradual yellowing, drying of leaves, hollow stalk. Crop rotation + resistant varieties.

### Major Pests
- **Early Shoot Borer:** Dead hearts in young crop. Release Trichogramma chilonis egg cards (50,000/ha/release, 6 releases). Spray Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC @ 0.3 ml/L.
- **Top Borer:** Dead hearts in later stages, bunchy top. Light traps during emergence (March–April). Spray Fipronil 5% SC @ 2 ml/L.
- **Woolly Aphid:** White waxy growth on leaf undersurface. Release predator Dipha aphidivora. Spray Imidacloprid 17.8% SL @ 0.3 ml/L.

## Soybean (सोयाबीन)

### Recommended Varieties
- **Madhya Pradesh:** JS-335, JS-9560, JS-9752
- **Maharashtra:** MAUS-71, MAUS-162
- **Rajasthan:** JS-335, RKS-24

### Sowing Protocol
- **Season:** June 20 – July 10 (with monsoon onset)
- **Seed rate:** 60–80 kg/ha
- **Spacing:** 30–45 cm × 5–7 cm
- **Seed treatment:** Trichoderma viride @ 4 g/kg + Rhizobium japonicum culture @ 5 g/kg (10 min gap between treatments)

### Major Diseases
- **Yellow Mosaic Virus (YMV):** Bright yellow patches on leaves, transmitted by whitefly. No direct chemical control. Control whitefly vector. Use resistant varieties (JS-335 is moderately resistant).
- **Anthracnose (Colletotrichum truncatum):** Dark sunken lesions on stems and pods. Seed treatment + Carbendazim 50% WP @ 1 g/L spray at flowering.
- **Rust (Phakopsora pachyrhizi):** Red-brown pustules on leaf undersurface. Spray Hexaconazole 5% SC @ 2 ml/L.

### Major Pests
- **Girdle Beetle:** Girdles stem, causing breakage. Seed treatment with Thiamethoxam 30% FS @ 10 ml/kg. Spray Profenophos 50% EC @ 2 ml/L.
- **Stem Fly:** Tunneling in stem, wilting. Early sowing + Imidacloprid seed treatment.
- **Green Semilooper / Tobacco Caterpillar:** Defoliation. Spray Quinalphos 25% EC @ 2 ml/L or NPV (Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus) @ 250 LE/ha.

## Maize (मक्का)

### Recommended Varieties
- **Hybrid:** DHM-117, HQPM-1, Vivek QPM-9
- **Composite:** Navjot, Pusa Early Hybrid Makka-5
- **Sweet Corn:** Sugar-75, Madhuri

### Sowing Protocol
- **Kharif:** June–July (main season)
- **Rabi:** October–November (peninsular India)
- **Spring:** January–February (Bihar, W. Bengal)
- **Seed rate:** 20 kg/ha (hybrid), 25 kg/ha (composite)
- **Spacing:** 60–75 cm × 20–25 cm

### Major Diseases
- **Turcicum Leaf Blight (Exserohilum turcicum):** Long elliptical grey-green lesions on leaves. Spray Mancozeb 75% WP @ 2.5 g/L.
- **Maydis Leaf Blight (Bipolaris maydis):** Small, rectangular, tan lesions. Spray Zineb 75% WP @ 2.5 g/L.
- **Downy Mildew (Peronosclerospora sorghi):** Chlorotic streaks on leaves, white downy growth. Seed treatment with Metalaxyl 35% WS @ 6 g/kg.

### Major Pests
- **Fall Armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda):** Ragged feeding damage on whorl leaves, frass visible. Spray Emamectin Benzoate 5% SG @ 0.4 g/L or Spinetoram 11.7% SC @ 0.5 ml/L. Apply in late evening.
- **Stem Borer:** Dead heart in vegetative stage. Spray Fipronil 5% SC @ 2 ml/L in whorl.
- **Cob Borer:** Damage to developing kernels. Apply Trichogramma cards (50,000/ha).

## Soil Health Management

### Soil Testing Frequency
- **Recommended:** Once every 2–3 cropping cycles
- **Sampling depth:** 0–15 cm (general), 15–30 cm (deep-rooted crops)
- **Sampling time:** After harvest, before next season fertilizer application
- **Samples per field:** Minimum 8–10 random spots, mix to create composite sample

### Soil Moisture Thresholds (for AgroSense IoT)
| Soil Type | Wilting Point (%) | Field Capacity (%) | Saturation (%) | Optimal Range (%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sandy | 5–8 | 12–18 | 35–40 | 10–16 |
| Sandy Loam | 8–12 | 18–25 | 40–45 | 15–22 |
| Loam | 12–17 | 25–35 | 45–50 | 20–30 |
| Clay Loam | 15–22 | 30–40 | 48–55 | 25–35 |
| Clay | 20–30 | 35–45 | 50–60 | 30–40 |
| Black Cotton | 22–35 | 38–50 | 55–65 | 33–45 |

### Organic Carbon Guidelines
- **Low:** < 0.5% → Apply FYM @ 10–15 t/ha + Green Manuring
- **Medium:** 0.5–0.75% → Apply FYM @ 5–10 t/ha
- **High:** > 0.75% → Maintenance dose of FYM @ 5 t/ha

### pH-Based Liming/Gypsum Recommendations
- **pH < 5.5 (Strongly Acidic):** Apply agricultural lime @ 2–4 t/ha
- **pH 5.5–6.5 (Slightly Acidic):** Apply lime @ 1–2 t/ha
- **pH 6.5–7.5 (Neutral):** No amendment needed
- **pH 7.5–8.5 (Slightly Alkaline):** Apply Gypsum @ 2–5 t/ha
- **pH > 8.5 (Strongly Alkaline):** Apply Gypsum @ 5–10 t/ha + Pyrite

## Integrated Pest Management (IPM) Principles

### Decision Thresholds
Before recommending any pesticide, verify:
1. **Pest identification is confirmed** (not assumed from symptoms alone)
2. **Economic Threshold Level (ETL) is crossed** (pest population justifies chemical intervention)
3. **Non-chemical methods tried first:** Cultural (crop rotation, resistant varieties), Mechanical (hand picking, traps), Biological (Trichogramma, NPV, predators)
4. **Chemical is CIBRC-registered** for the target crop-pest combination
5. **Chemical is NOT on the CIBRC banned list**

### Spray Timing
- **Best time:** Early morning (6–8 AM) or late evening (4–6 PM)
- **Avoid:** Spraying during rain, strong wind (>10 km/h), or peak sun
- **Pre-harvest interval:** Follow the specific PHI for each chemical (do not spray close to harvest)
- **Water volume:** 500 L/ha (knapsack), 200 L/ha (power sprayer), 25–50 L/ha (drone)

### Safety Reminders for Farmers
- Wear protective equipment: mask, gloves, full-body clothing
- Do not eat, drink, or smoke during spraying
- Wash hands and face with soap after spraying
- Keep children and animals away from sprayed fields for 24 hours
- Dispose of empty containers safely — triple-rinse and puncture

## Weather-Based Advisory Logic

### Temperature Thresholds for Common Crops
| Crop | Frost Damage (<°C) | Optimal Min (°C) | Optimal Max (°C) | Heat Stress (>°C) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wheat | 0 | 10 | 25 | 35 |
| Paddy | 10 | 22 | 32 | 40 |
| Potato | -1 | 15 | 25 | 30 |
| Cotton | 5 | 20 | 35 | 42 |
| Maize | 5 | 18 | 33 | 40 |

### Humidity-Disease Correlation Rules
- **Humidity > 85% for 48+ hours → HIGH RISK for:**
  - Late Blight (Potato, Tomato)
  - Blast (Rice)
  - Downy Mildew (Maize, Pearl Millet)
  - Grey Mildew (Cotton)
- **Humidity > 80% + Temperature 15–25°C → HIGH RISK for:**
  - Yellow Rust (Wheat)
  - Powdery Mildew (Wheat, Pea)
- **Action:** Issue preventive spray advisory BEFORE disease appears when weather conditions match risk profile. Do NOT wait for symptoms.

### Rainfall-Based Irrigation Suppression
- **If IMD forecast shows >20 mm rain in next 48 hours:** Suppress irrigation alert
- **If cumulative rainfall >50 mm in past 7 days:** Skip next scheduled irrigation
- **If rainfall <10 mm in past 14 days in Kharif:** Issue water stress warning

## CIBRC Safety Protocol

### Before Recommending Any Chemical
1. Check if the chemical is in the **CIBRC Banned List** → NEVER recommend
2. Check if the chemical is **CIBRC Restricted** → Recommend with usage restrictions noted
3. Verify the chemical is **CIBRC Registered** for the specific crop-pest/disease combination
4. Include the **recommended dosage** from ICAR Package of Practices
5. Include the **Pre-Harvest Interval (PHI)**
6. Include **safety precautions** for the farmer

### Known Banned Chemicals (as of March 2026)
This is a subset — always cross-check with the full `cibrc_database.csv`:
- Endosulfan
- Chlorpyrifos (restricted/banned in several formulations)
- Monocrotophos
- Methyl Parathion
- Dicofol
- Dinocap
- Methomyl
- Carbaryl (restricted)
- Phorate (restricted)
- Triazophos (restricted)
