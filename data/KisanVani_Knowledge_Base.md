# Kisan Vani: Agricultural Knowledge Base
**Voice Advisory System — Prototype KB v1.0**

This document is the primary knowledge base for the Kisan Vani IVR voice assistant. It contains ICAR-standard crop disease protocols, safe chemical recommendations, and the complete CIBRC banned/restricted chemical registry as of 2024. 

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The agent must prioritise information in this document over general knowledge. If a farmer asks about a **BANNED** chemical (Section 3), refuse and suggest a safe alternative. Never recommend dosages not listed here.

## 1. Crop Disease Protocols

### Wheat (*Triticum aestivum*)
**Season:** Rabi (Oct–Mar)

#### 1. Yellow Rust / Stripe Rust
- **Pathogen:** *Puccinia striiformis*
- **Severity:** HIGH — can cause 70% yield loss if untreated
- **Symptoms:** Yellow-orange stripes along leaf veins; severe in cool humid weather (10-15°C); spreads rapidly in Punjab, Haryana, UP during Feb-Mar.
- **Treatment:**
  - Propiconazole 25% EC — 0.1% spray (1 ml per litre water).
  - Tebuconazole 250 EW — 1 ml per litre.
  - Spray at first sign, repeat after 15 days if needed.
- **Prevention:** Avoid overhead irrigation when humidity >80%. Use resistant varieties: HD-2967, PBW-550.

#### 2. Brown Rust / Leaf Rust
- **Pathogen:** *Puccinia triticina*
- **Severity:** MEDIUM — up to 40% yield loss
- **Symptoms:** Circular orange-brown pustules on upper leaf surface; appears Feb-Mar when temp 15-22°C.
- **Treatment:**
  - Mancozeb 75% WP — 2.5 g per litre.
  - Propiconazole 25% EC — 1 ml per litre.
  - Spray when 5% leaf area infected.
- **Prevention:** Remove infected stubble after harvest. Avoid late sowing.

#### 3. Loose Smut
- **Pathogen:** *Ustilago tritici*
- **Severity:** LOW (if seed treated) — HIGH (if untreated, 30-40% loss)
- **Symptoms:** Entire ear replaced by black powder (smut spores).
- **Treatment (Seed Treatment):**
  - Carboxin 37.5% + Thiram 37.5% DS — 3 g per kg seed.
  - Tebuconazole 2% DS — 1.5 g per kg seed before sowing.
- **Prevention:** Use certified disease-free seed. Do not save smutted grain for sowing.

#### 4. Powdery Mildew
- **Symptoms:** White powdery patches on leaves, stems, ears.
- **Treatment:**
  - Propiconazole 25% EC — 1 ml per litre.
  - Hexaconazole 5% SC — 1.5 ml per litre.
  - Sulphur 80% WP — 2.5 g per litre.

#### 5. Karnal Bunt
- **Symptoms:** Partial bunting of wheat grains; fishy smell; kernel partially replaced by black mass.
- **Treatment:**
  - Seed treatment: Carboxin 75% WP — 2.5 g per kg seed.
  - Foliar: Propiconazole 25% EC — 1 ml per litre at ear emergence.

#### 6. Alternaria Leaf Blight
- **Symptoms:** Dark brown oval spots with yellow halo; tip dieback.
- **Treatment:**
  - Mancozeb 75% WP — 2.5 g per litre.
  - Zineb 75% WP — 2 g per litre.

### Rice (*Oryza sativa*)
**Season:** Kharif (Jun–Nov)

#### 1. Blast
- **Pathogen:** *Pyricularia oryzae*
- **Severity:** VERY HIGH — up to 100% loss
- **Symptoms:** Diamond-shaped grey-white lesions with brown border; neck blast causes panicle drop.
- **Treatment:**
  - Tricyclazole 75% WP — 0.6 g per litre.
  - Carbendazim 50% WP — 1 g per litre.
  - Isoprothiolane 40% EC — 1.5 ml per litre.
- **Prevention:** Use resistant varieties (MTU-1010, Pusa Basmati-1). Avoid excess nitrogen.

#### 2. Brown Plant Hopper (BPH)
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Symptoms:** 'Hopper burn' — circular yellowing patches; plants collapse from base.
- **Treatment:**
  - Buprofezin 25% SC — 1.25 ml per litre (nymph stage).
  - Pymetrozine 50% WG — 0.3 g per litre.
  - Thiamethoxam 25% WG — 0.3 g per litre.

#### 3. Sheath Blight
- **Symptoms:** Oval grey lesions with brown border on leaf sheaths near waterline.
- **Treatment:**
  - Hexaconazole 5% SC — 2 ml per litre.
  - Propiconazole 25% EC — 1 ml per litre.
  - Validamycin 3% L — 2.5 ml per litre.

#### 4. Bacterial Leaf Blight (BLB)
- **Symptoms:** Water-soaked margins turning yellow then white; 'kresek' (wilting) in seedlings.
- **Treatment:** No chemical cure. Spray Copper Oxychloride 50% WP — 3 g per litre as protectant.

#### 5. False Smut
- **Symptoms:** Grains replaced by orange-green powdery balls.
- **Treatment:** Propiconazole 25% EC — 1 ml per litre at boot to heading stage.

### Cotton (*Gossypium hirsutum*)
**Season:** Kharif (Apr–Oct)

#### 1. Cotton Bollworm / American Bollworm
- **Severity:** VERY HIGH
- **Treatment:**
  - Emamectin Benzoate 5% SG — 0.4 g per litre.
  - Spinosad 45% SC — 0.3 ml per litre.
  - Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC — 0.3 ml per litre.

#### 2. Whitefly & Leaf Curl Virus
- **Status:** Virus vector control is critical; no cure for infected plants.
- **Treatment:**
  - Thiamethoxam 25% WG — 0.3 g per litre.
  - Diafenthiuron 50% WP — 1.5 g per litre.
  - Pyriproxyfen 10% EC — 1 ml per litre.

---

### Tomato (*Lycopersicon esculentum*)
**Season:** Year-round

#### 1. Early Blight
- **Symptoms:** Concentric rings (target board pattern) on older leaves first.
- **Treatment:** Mancozeb 75% WP (2.5g/L) or Azoxystrobin 23% SC (1ml/L).

#### 2. Late Blight
- **Severity:** VERY HIGH — can destroy crop in days.
- **Treatment:**
  - Metalaxyl 8% + Mancozeb 64% WP — 2.5 g per litre.
  - Cymoxanil 8% + Mancozeb 64% WP — 3 g per litre.

#### 3. Tomato Leaf Curl Virus (ToLCV)
- **Rule:** No cure; control whitefly vector with Imidacloprid or Thiamethoxam.

#### 4. Damping Off (Nursery)
- **Treatment:** Drench beds with Metalaxyl-M or Carbendazim.

---

### Soybean (*Glycine max*)
- **Yellow Mosaic Virus (YMV):** No cure; control whitefly vector. Use MAUS-81 or NRC-37 tolerant varieties.
- **Stem Fly:** Seed treatment with Thiamethoxam 70% WS.
- **Pod Borer:** Quinalphos 25% EC or Lambda-cyhalothrin 5% EC at pod fill.

---

### Maize (*Zea mays*)
- **Fall Armyworm (FAW):** **Priority Pest.** Use Emamectin Benzoate 5% SG or Spinetoram 11.7% SC. Pour solution into whorl.
- **Turcicum Leaf Blight:** Mancozeb 75% WP or Propiconazole 25% EC.
- **Stem Borer:** Chlorpyrifos 10% CG in whorl. (Carofuran is banned).

---

### Sugarcane (*Saccharum officinarum*)
- **Red Rot:** **Very High Severity.** No curative spray. Use resistant varieties (Co-0238). Treat setts with Carbendazim.
- **Pyrilla:** Dimethoate 30% EC or Quinalphos 25% EC.
- **Smut:** Rogue shoots immediately. Hot water treat setts.

---

### Onion (*Allium cepa*)
- **Purple Blotch:** Mancozeb 75% WP + sticker or Difenoconazole 25% EC.
- **Thrips:** Very high loss risk. Use Spinosad 45% SC or Fipronil 5% SC.
- **Basal Rot:** Seed treatment with Carbendazim.

## 5. Soil Moisture & Irrigation Advisory

| Crop | Optimal Moisture % | Stress Below % | Waterlogging Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wheat** | 18–35% | 10% | Above 40% |
| **Rice** | 60–100% | 50% | Over 105% |
| **Cotton** | 40–70% | 20% | Above 75% |
| **Tomato** | 50–80% | 30% | Above 85% |
| **Sugarcane** | 55–80% | 35% | Above 85% |
| **Soybean** | 40–70% | 25% | Above 75% |
| **Maize** | 45–75% | 25% | Above 80% |

### General Decision Rules
- **Irrigate** if soil moisture < stress threshold AND no rain forecast for 3 days.
- **Delay** if moisture > optimal following rain.
- **Urgent irrigation** if Soil Temp > 35°C AND moisture < 40%.
- **Disease Alert** if Humidity > 85% for 3 days.
